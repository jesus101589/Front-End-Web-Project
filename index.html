The Lone Star State

As of 2024, the state of Texas has a population of 30,976,754 with a growth rate of 1.80% according to the Census which makes Texas the third ranked state in the nation for population growth. This ranks Texas third in the nation for population increase. Geographically, Texas is approximately 268,596 square miles making it the second largest state within the United States! In the year 1845, Texas was annexed into the United States of America making it the twenty-eighth state in the Union. The state capital of Texas is Austin which is located in the central region. Tex-Mex, tacos, and barbecue are some of the best food you can have in the state!

Pecan Tree - The Official State Tree of Texas

Rumor has it that James Stephen Hogg made a special request to have a pecan tree over his grave. As a result, the native tree rose in popularity. It was declared the state tree in 1919. Widespread in the wild and popular as a yard tree, the pecan is native to Texas and produces the state's only commercially grown nut.
